Exploring Geometry

Lines and Angles

The building blocks of geometry are lines and angles. Angles are formed when two lines meet and are measured in degrees (°).

  • Acute Angle: Less than 90°.
  • Right Angle: Exactly 90°.
  • Obtuse Angle: Greater than 90°.
  • Complementary Angles: Two angles that add up to 90°.
  • Supplementary Angles: Two angles that add up to 180°.

The World of Triangles

A key property of triangles is that their three interior angles always add up to 180°. They are classified as:

  • Equilateral: All three sides and angles are equal.
  • Isosceles: Two sides and two angles are equal.
  • Scalene: No sides or angles are equal.
