Organic Chemistry Basics

Functional Groups üåø

A functional group is a specific group of atoms in a molecule that is responsible for its characteristic chemical reactions. They are the most important part of organic chemistry!

  • Alcohol (-OH): Contains a hydroxyl group.
  • Aldehyde (-CHO): Contains a carbonyl group at the end of a chain.
  • Ketone (C=O): Contains a carbonyl group in the middle of a chain.
  • Carboxylic Acid (-COOH): Contains a carboxyl group.

How to Play the Games

  • Easy (Detective): Click on the molecules with the target functional group as they fly by.
  • Medium (Conveyor): Sort molecules by their functional group into the correct bins.
  • Hard (Reaction Chain): Apply the correct chemical reactions to transform one molecule into another.
