Coal & Petroleum

Fossil Fuels ⛽

Coal, petroleum, and natural gas are fossil fuels. They were formed from the dead remains of living organisms millions of years ago. They are non-renewable, meaning they will run out one day.

Renewable vs. Non-Renewable

Energy sources can be classified into two groups:

  • Renewable 🌱: These sources can be replenished naturally. Examples include solar, wind, and hydropower.
  • Non-Renewable ⛽: These sources are finite and will be exhausted. Examples include coal, petroleum, and natural gas.

How to Play the Games

  • Easy (Sorter): Drag resources into 'Renewable' or 'Non-Renewable' bins.
  • Medium (Distillation Tower): Drag petroleum fractions to their correct tray in the tower.
  • Hard (Pollution Simulator): Burn different fuels and observe their impact on the environment.
