Force & Motion

Force and motion are fundamental concepts in physics that describe how objects move and interact with each other.

Newton's Laws of Motion

  1. First Law (Inertia): An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion, unless acted upon by a force.
  2. Second Law: The acceleration of an object is proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass (F = ma).
  3. Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
