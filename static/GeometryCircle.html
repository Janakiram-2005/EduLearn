Circle Theorems

Key Parts of a Circle

  • Radius: A line from the center to the circumference.
  • Chord: A line connecting two points on the circumference. A diameter is the longest chord.
  • Tangent: A line that touches the circumference at exactly one point.
  • Arc: A part of the circumference.

Important Theorems

  • Angle at the Center: Is double the angle at the circumference from the same arc.
  • Angles in the Same Segment: Are equal.
  • Tangent-Radius Theorem: The angle between a tangent and a radius is 90°.
  • Cyclic Quadrilateral: Opposite angles add up to 180°.
