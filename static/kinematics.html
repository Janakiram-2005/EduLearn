Introduction to Kinematics

1D Motion

Kinematics is the study of motion. In one-dimensional (1D) motion, we look at objects moving in a straight line. The key equation is: s = ut + ¬Ωat¬≤, where 's' is displacement, 'u' is initial velocity, 'a' is acceleration, and 't' is time.

2D Motion (Projectiles)

Objects moving in two dimensions, like a ball thrown through the air, follow a curved path called a parabola. We analyze their horizontal (x) and vertical (y) motion separately.

Motion Graphs

We can represent motion visually using graphs:

  • Position-Time (s-t): A straight line means constant velocity. A curve means acceleration.
  • Velocity-Time (v-t): A horizontal line means constant velocity. A sloped line means constant acceleration. The area under the graph gives the displacement.
