Combustion & Flame

What is Combustion?

Combustion is a chemical process where a substance reacts with oxygen to produce heat and light. The substance that burns is a fuel.

Types of Combustion

  • Complete Combustion: Occurs with plenty of oxygen. Produces a clean, hot, blue flame (e.g., Gas stove).
  • Incomplete Combustion: Occurs with limited oxygen. Produces a cooler, yellow/orange flame and soot (e.g., Candle flame).

Fire Safety

The Fire Triangle requires: Heat, Fuel, and Oxygen. Removing any one extinguishes the fire.

  • CO₂ Extinguishers are best for electrical fires.
  • Covering a fire with a blanket cuts off the oxygen supply.
