Calculus: Limits & Derivatives

Prepare for the calculus challenge by reviewing these fundamental concepts and rules.

Key Concepts

  • Limit: The value that a function approaches as the input gets closer to a certain point. It's about the journey, not the destination itself. Denoted as $ \lim_{x \to c} f(x) $.
  • Derivative: Measures the instantaneous rate of change or the slope of a function at a specific point. Denoted as $f'(x)$ or $\frac{d}{dx}f(x)$.

Fundamental Rules

  • Power Rule: $\frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}$
  • Product Rule: $\frac{d}{dx}(uv) = u'v + uv'$
  • Quotient Rule: $\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{u}{v}\right) = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$
  • Chain Rule: $\frac{d}{dx}(f(g(x))) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$
