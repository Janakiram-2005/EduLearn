Introduction to Graphs

The Cartesian Plane

A graph is often drawn on a Cartesian Plane, which is like a map. It has two main lines:

  • The x-axis, which runs horizontally (left to right).
  • The y-axis, which runs vertically (up and down).
  • The point where they meet (0,0) is called the Origin.

Plotting Coordinates

A point on the graph is identified by coordinates, written as (x, y). The first number (x) tells you how far to go along the x-axis, and the second number (y) tells you how far to go up or down the y-axis.

Example: To plot the point (3, 4), you start at the origin, move 3 units to the right, and then 4 units up.
