Electrochemistry

What is Electrochemistry? ⚡

Electrochemistry is the study of chemical reactions that produce or are caused by electricity. It's the science behind batteries, fuel cells, and processes like electroplating.

Key Concepts

  • Galvanic Cell (Battery): A device that converts chemical energy into electrical energy using two different metals (electrodes) and salt solutions (electrolytes).
  • Redox Reactions: Reactions involving the transfer of electrons. Oxidation is the loss of electrons, and Reduction is the gain of electrons.
  • Electroplating: A process that uses electric current to coat an object with a thin layer of metal.

How to Play the Games

  • Easy (Battery Builder): Select two different electrodes to create a working battery and measure its voltage.
  • Medium (Redox Battle): Determine which substance is oxidized and which is reduced in a reaction.
  • Hard (Electroplating): Adjust current and time to perfectly plate an object with a new metal layer.
