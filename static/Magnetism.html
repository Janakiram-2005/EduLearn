Magnetism: Properties and Applications

Magnetism is a fundamental force of nature that causes materials to attract or repel each other. It arises from the motion of electric charges.

Basic Principles

  • Magnetic Poles: All magnets have north and south poles. Like poles repel, opposite poles attract.
  • Magnetic Field: The region around a magnet where its force acts.
  • Magnetic Materials: Materials strongly attracted to magnets, like iron, nickel, and cobalt.

Types of Magnets

TypeDescription
Permanent MagnetsRetain their magnetic properties.
Temporary MagnetsAct as magnets only when in a magnetic field.
ElectromagnetsCreated by an electric current.
