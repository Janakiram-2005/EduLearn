Proportions Explained

Direct Proportion

Two quantities are in direct proportion when they increase or decrease together at the same rate. If you double one quantity, the other one also doubles.

Example: The more hours you work, the more money you earn. If you buy more packets of chips, the total cost increases.

Inverse Proportion

Two quantities are in inverse proportion when an increase in one quantity causes a decrease in the other, and vice versa.

Example: The more people you have to do a job, the less time it will take. If you increase the speed of a car, it takes less time to reach your destination.
