Trigonometric Functions

Key Concepts

  • Trigonometric Ratios: Relationships between angles and side lengths in a right-angled triangle (sin, cos, tan, csc, sec, cot).
  • Unit Circle: A circle with a radius of 1, used to define trigonometric values for all angles. [Image of a unit circle with angles]
  • Fundamental Identity: The most famous identity is $\sin^2(x) + \cos^2(x) = 1$.
  • Periodicity: Trigonometric functions are periodic, meaning their graphs repeat in regular intervals. For example, the period of $\sin(x)$ is $2\pi$.
