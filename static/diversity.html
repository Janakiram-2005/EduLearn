👑 The Five Kingdoms of Life

In biology, organisms are classified into broad groups called kingdoms. This system helps scientists understand the relationships between different forms of life.

The Five Kingdoms

  • Monera: Unicellular organisms that lack a nucleus (prokaryotes). E.g., bacteria.
  • Protista: Mostly unicellular organisms that have a nucleus (eukaryotes). E.g., amoeba, algae.
  • Fungi: Organisms that absorb nutrients from others. E.g., mushrooms, yeast.
  • Plantae: Multicellular organisms that make their own food (autotrophs). E.g., trees, ferns.
  • Animalia: Multicellular organisms that consume other organisms (heterotrophs). E.g., lions, worms.
