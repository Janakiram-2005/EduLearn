Heat: Transfer and Effects

Heat is a form of energy that is transferred between objects with different temperatures. It always flows from a hotter object to a cooler one.

Methods of Heat Transfer

  • Conduction: Transfer through direct contact.
  • Convection: Transfer through the movement of fluids (liquids or gases).
  • Radiation: Transfer through electromagnetic waves.

Effects of Heat

EffectDescription
ExpansionMost substances expand when heated.
Change of StateSolid to liquid (melting), liquid to gas (boiling).
Temperature ChangeIncrease in molecular kinetic energy.
