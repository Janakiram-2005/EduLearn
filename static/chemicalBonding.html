A chemical bond is a lasting attraction between atoms that enables the formation of chemical compounds. Atoms form bonds to achieve a more stable electron configuration, usually by having a full outer shell of electrons.

Types of Bonds

  • Ionic Bond: Formed when one atom *transfers* one or more electrons to another atom. This typically happens between a metal (like Na) and a non-metal (like Cl).
  • Covalent Bond: Formed when atoms *share* one or more pairs of electrons. This typically happens between two non-metal atoms (like H and O to form water).

How to Play the Games

  • Easy (Quiz Challenge): Answer questions about bonding types.
  • Medium (Matching Game): Match compounds to their bond type (Ionic or Covalent).
  • Hard (Molecule Builder): Drag and drop atoms to build molecules.
