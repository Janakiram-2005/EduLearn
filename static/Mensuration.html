Surface Area & Volume

What's the Difference?

In mensuration, we measure geometric figures. Two key measurements for 3D shapes are surface area and volume.

  • Surface Area: Think of it as the "wrapping paper." It's the total area of all the surfaces (or faces) of a 3D object. It's measured in square units (like cm²).
  • Volume: Think of it as the "space inside." It's the amount of space an object occupies. It's measured in cubic units (like cm³).

Common Formulas

Here are the formulas for some common shapes (l=length, w=width, h=height, r=radius):

Cube (side 's'):

  • Volume = s³
  • Surface Area = 6s²

Cuboid:

  • Volume = l × w × h
  • Surface Area = 2(lw + lh + wh)
