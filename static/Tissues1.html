Tissues are groups of cells that have a similar structure and act together to perform a specific function. There are four main types of tissue in animals and several types in plants.

Plant Tissues

  • Xylem: Transports water from roots to other parts.
  • Phloem: Transports food from leaves to other parts.
  • Parenchyma: Stores food and performs photosynthesis.
  • Collenchyma: Provides flexible support to plant parts.
  • Sclerenchyma: Provides rigid strength to plants.

Animal Tissues

  • Epithelial: Covers body surfaces and lines internal organs.
  • Connective: Supports, connects, and binds other tissues. (e.g., bone, blood)
  • Muscular: Composed of cells that can contract to cause movement.
  • Nervous: Transmits electrical signals between the brain, spinal cord, and nerves.
